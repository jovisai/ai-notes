From Business Goals to Code with Backward Thinking

23 Sep 2025

Stop Building Features, Start Driving Outcomes

The most impactful shift an engineering manager can make is to move from a “feature-first” to a “business-outcome-first” mindset. Instead of asking “What should we build?”, start by asking “What business result do we need to achieve?” This is the essence of working backward.

This approach reframes your team’s purpose from writing code to solving business problems, directly connecting their work to the company’s success.

The 4-Step Backward-Thinking Framework:

  1. Define the Business Win: Start at the end. What is the specific, measurable business outcome you need to create? Don’t state a feature. State the goal.

    • Instead of: “We need to build a new search results page.”
    • Think: “We need to increase user engagement on our platform by 15% this quarter.”

  2. Identify the Customer Impact: How must the customer’s experience or behavior change to achieve that business win? This links the abstract goal to a tangible human action.

    • Think: “To increase engagement, users must be able to find relevant content within 5 seconds of landing on the page.”

  3. Architect the Technical Solution: Now, and only now, design the engineering work. What is the simplest, most effective technical solution that will deliver the required customer impact? This is where you discuss features, architecture, and implementation.

    • Think: “To enable 5-second content discovery, we need to implement a real-time recommendation engine and a highly optimized, low-latency search API.”

  4. Measure and Iterate: Ship the solution, but measure its success against the initial business goal (user engagement), not just engineering metrics (API response time). Did the metric move? If not, iterate on the solution.

By consistently applying this framework, you transform your team from a cost center that ships features into a strategic partner that drives measurable business growth and builds a culture of true ownership.
